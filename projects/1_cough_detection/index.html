On-Device Cough Detection & Respiratory Disease Classification

Smartphone-based cough detection and asthma/COPD screening from tracheal lung sounds (Pfizer CDI — VBD-1 & VBD-2).

Designed, developed, and deployed smartphone-based services for cough detection, respiratory disease analysis, and continuous screening using tracheal-acquired lung sound recordings. The pipeline runs in real time on commodity Android hardware using the built-in microphone, with abnormal/normal lung-sound classification and diagnostic models that distinguish asthma from COPD based on acoustic biomarkers and patient clinical history.

Developed under the externally funded Pfizer Collaborative Development Initiative (CDI) Voice-Based Diagnostics contracts (VBD-1 and VBD-2) at the University of Patras.

Related publications: (Kontogiannis et al., 2026; Kontogiannis et al., 2026).

    On-device Cough Detection and Respiratory Disease Classification Enhanced by Generative Data Augmentation
    Georgios Kontogiannis, Pantelis Tzamalis, Anastases Giannikopoulos, and 1 more author
    Computers in Biology and Medicine, 2026
    In Press
    Abs Bib

    Cough sounds are accessible, non-invasive biomarkers for respiratory disease assessment capturable using consumer-grade smartphones. We develop a multilayer, smartphone-compatible AI framework for automated cough detection and respiratory disease classification, and propose a generative augmentation strategy utilizing five Variational Autoencoder variants with a probabilistic cough-level fusion mechanism to address training data scarcity. The framework comprises a Cough Detection Module for real-time segmentation, a Disease Analysis Module classifying asthma, COVID-19, or healthy coughs, and a Generative Augmentation Module operating across the time-frequency domain while reconstructing samples in the time-domain for acoustic verifiability. All components operate in real time on commodity Android hardware.
