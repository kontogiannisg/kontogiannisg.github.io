I develop AI systems that turn everyday devices—smartphones and wearables—into reliable, clinical-grade diagnostic tools. My work sits at the intersection of **audio AI**, **deep generative modeling**, and **edge-deployed machine learning**, anchored in real-world healthcare settings through close clinical and industry collaborations. The guiding question across my research is how to make machine learning models that are not merely accurate on benchmarks, but robust, trustworthy, and deployable under the messy conditions of actual clinical practice.

<div class="card mt-3 p-3">
  <h3 class="card-title font-weight-medium">Audio AI &amp; Respiratory Sound Analysis</h3>
  <p>
    My core line of work studies digital signal processing and deep learning for respiratory and cough sound analysis. I design models that detect coughs and classify respiratory conditions (e.g., asthma vs. COPD) directly from tracheal-acquired lung sounds captured on commodity smartphones. A recurring theme is moving auscultation out of the clinic: building pipelines accurate and lightweight enough to run on-device, in real time, without specialized hardware.
  </p>
</div>

<div class="card mt-3 p-3">
  <h3 class="card-title font-weight-medium">Deep Generative Modeling for Medical Audio</h3>
  <p>
    Clinical audio datasets are small, imbalanced, and expensive to label. I develop deep generative models that synthesize clinically meaningful cough and lung sounds, using them as a principled data-augmentation strategy to improve classifier robustness. This includes generative architectures that operate across the time-frequency domain while reconstructing acoustically verifiable samples in the time domain.
  </p>
</div>

<div class="card mt-3 p-3">
  <h3 class="card-title font-weight-medium">Robust &amp; Deployment-Aware Machine Learning</h3>
  <p>
    Models that excel in the lab routinely fail in deployment because of domain shift and dataset heterogeneity—different recording devices, auscultation sites, and patient populations introduce systematic variability unrelated to the underlying pathology. I study metadata-conditioning and domain-adaptation mechanisms that let a single model adapt to these conditions, with particular attention to underrepresented patient subgroups, explainability, and trustworthy behavior in real-world healthcare systems.
  </p>
</div>

<div class="card mt-3 p-3">
  <h3 class="card-title font-weight-medium">Multimodal &amp; Clinical AI</h3>
  <p>
    Working with the University General Hospital of Patras and within the EU Horizon <a href="https://www.synairg.eu/" target="_blank">SynAir-G</a> project, I build multimodal models that combine paired lung-sound recordings, chest imaging, and inflammatory biomarkers (e.g., to distinguish bacterial vs. viral pneumonia), and I study causal relationships between environmental signals, wearable physiology, and health outcomes. Through the <a href="https://www.pfizer.com/" target="_blank">Pfizer CDI</a> EyeAI project, I also work on computer vision for ocular biomarkers, including sclera segmentation and non-invasive jaundice assessment from smartphone images.
  </p>
</div>

<div class="card mt-3 p-3">
  <h3 class="card-title font-weight-medium">Graph-Based Information Retrieval</h3>
  <p>
    Originating in my MSc thesis, I work on graph-based extensions of set-based information retrieval, improving retrieval quality through spectral clustering and embedding-driven query expansion. This thread informs my broader interest in structured representations and how learned embeddings can be combined with graph structure.
  </p>
</div>

<div class="card mt-3 p-3">
  <h3 class="card-title font-weight-medium">Future Directions</h3>
  <p>
    I aim to push edge-deployed medical AI from proof-of-concept toward dependable clinical tools: tighter integration of generative augmentation with robust training, multimodal fusion that remains calibrated under real-world shift, and diagnostic systems whose decisions are explainable and accountable to clinicians. I am open to post-doctoral opportunities, research collaborations in audio and medical AI, and industry research roles in applied machine learning.
  </p>
</div>
