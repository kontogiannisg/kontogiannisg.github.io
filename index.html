selected publications

  1. CBM
    On-device Cough Detection and Respiratory Disease Classification Enhanced by Generative Data Augmentation
    Georgios Kontogiannis, Pantelis Tzamalis, Anastases Giannikopoulos, and 1 more author
    Computers in Biology and Medicine, 2026
    In Press
    Abs Bib

    Cough sounds are accessible, non-invasive biomarkers for respiratory disease assessment capturable using consumer-grade smartphones. We develop a multilayer, smartphone-compatible AI framework for automated cough detection and respiratory disease classification, and propose a generative augmentation strategy utilizing five Variational Autoencoder variants with a probabilistic cough-level fusion mechanism to address training data scarcity. The framework comprises a Cough Detection Module for real-time segmentation, a Disease Analysis Module classifying asthma, COVID-19, or healthy coughs, and a Generative Augmentation Module operating across the time-frequency domain while reconstructing samples in the time-domain for acoustic verifiability. All components operate in real time on commodity Android hardware.

  2. EUSIPCO
    Metadata-Conditioned Audio Transformers for Adaptive Respiratory Sound Classification
    Georgios Kontogiannis, Pantelis Tzamalis, and Sotiris Nikoletseas
    In Proceedings of the 34th European Signal Processing Conference (EUSIPCO), 2026
    In Press, Acceptance Rate: 50%
    Abs Bib

    Respiratory sound classification from auscultation recordings is confounded by heterogeneous acquisition conditions, including recording device, auscultation site, and patient characteristics, that introduce systematic variability unrelated to the underlying pathology. We propose a family of metadata-conditioning mechanisms for the Audio Spectrogram Transformer (AST), progressing from gated residual fusion to Feature-wise Linear Modulation (FiLM) adapted to the Transformer architecture, including two novel variants: Token-Aware FiLM (TAFiLM) and Soft-Factorized FiLM (SoftFiLM). Experiments on the ICBHI benchmark demonstrate consistent gains across all conditioning strategies. SoftFiLM achieves 64.11% on the official 4-class task (+3.94% over the baseline) and 72.40% on the 2-class task, establishing a new state-of-the-art on the 2-class task.
